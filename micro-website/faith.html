Foundations of Faith

What Christianity teaches

Christianity centers on the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ and the hope of salvation through Him.

God’s love

Christianity teaches that God created humanity with purpose and loves people deeply. His desire is for people to know Him, trust Him, and live in fellowship with Him.

The problem of sin

Sin separates people from God. The Bible teaches that everyone falls short and cannot save themselves through good works alone.

The gift of salvation

Jesus Christ died and rose again so that those who believe in Him may receive forgiveness, grace, and eternal life.

The gospel in simple terms

The gospel is the good news that Jesus Christ came into the world to save sinners. He lived a sinless life, died on the cross, and rose again. Through faith in Him, believers are forgiven and made new.

Christianity is not just a list of rules. It is a transformed life centered on Christ. Believers are called to love God, love others, grow in truth, and live with humility, compassion, and faith.

Key truths of the Christian faith

  • The Bible is God’s Word and teaches truth.
  • Jesus Christ is the Son of God and Savior of the world.
  • Salvation is by grace through faith.
  • Prayer is an important part of the Christian life.
  • Believers are called to live with love, holiness, and purpose.
